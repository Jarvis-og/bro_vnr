VNRVJIET Electrical and Electronics Engineering Infrastructure
Below is the categorized information for the laboratories under the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering.
There are 10 labs in the Department

1. Network Analysis Laboratory
Background: Provides the foundation for EEE, focusing on 3-phase and single-phase circuits. It is designed for active component analysis, including the design and testing of amplifiers and oscillators.

Description: Well-equipped for analog, digital, and mixed-signal circuit testing. It remains open after college hours for student projects.

Current Equipment: Analog and Digital Storage Oscilloscopes, VHF Function Generators, Power supplies, Decade boxes (R, L, C), 3-Phase Load/Supply, and PCs for system design.

Lab Investment: Rs. 10,14,856.45/-

Location: Room No. A-308

Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM

Contact: Mrs. K. Sravani (Asst. Prof) / Mrs. V. Malleswari (Instructor)

2. Electrical Machines Laboratory
Background: The core lab of the department where students get real-time experience with various electrical machines.

Description: Upgraded via AICTE MODROBS for data acquisition-based design. Supports regular academics, student projects, and research.

Current Equipment: BLDC motor, PMSM motor, DC/AC Motors and Generators, Transformers, and Ansys Maxwell software for machine design.

Lab Investment: Rs. 29,00,000/-

Location: Room No. S3-1

Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Contact: Mr. G.C. Prabhakar (Asst. Prof) / Mr. P.H. Syam Rao (Sr. Skilled Asst.)

3. Basic Electrical Engineering Laboratory
Background: Dedicated to undergraduate students across various branches (ECE, CSE, IT, ME, etc.) to learn fundamental electricity concepts.

Key Topics: Ohm’s Law, Kirchhoff’s Laws, Circuit Building (Breadboards), Measurement (Multimeters/Oscilloscopes), and Diode/Transistor characteristics.

Current Equipment: DC Compound motors, DC Shunt motor-generator sets, 3-phase squirrel cage induction motors, and 1-phase transformers.

Lab Investment: Rs. 10,37,791/-

Location: Room No. S1-2

Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Contact: Mr. N. Amarnadh Reddy (Asst. Prof) / P.H. Sham Rao (Sr. Skilled Asst.)

4. Power Electronics Lab
Background: Focuses on research and development for closed-loop control of power electronic converters (AC and DC).

Description: Includes study of SCR, MOSFET, and IGBT characteristics, as well as the operation of rectifiers, choppers, inverters, and cycloconverters.

Current Equipment: PWM inverters, Half/Full controlled rectifiers, MOSFET-based step-up/step-down choppers, and Digital Storage Oscilloscopes (DSO).

Lab Investment: Rs. 15,27,176/-

Location: Room No. A-310

Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM

Contact: Dr. G. Lakshminarayana (Asst. Prof) / Mr. M. Rama Krishna Das (Instructor)

5. Control Systems Laboratory
Background: Provides the foundation for control system study, analysis, and design, moving toward AI-based controllers.

Description: Equipped with specialized hardware like SCADA and Stepper motors for system study.

Current Equipment: PID Controller Kits, Magnetic Amplifiers, AC/DC Servo Motors, Lead-Lag Networks, Stepper motor with 8085 Mp kit, and Temperature Control Systems.

Lab Investment: Rs. 23,42,117/-

Location: Room No. A-309

Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM

Contact: Dr. G. Radhika (Sr. Asst. Prof) / V. Malleshwari (Instructor)

6. Simulation & Microprocessor Labs (Combined)
Simulation of Electrical Systems Laboratory
Scope: Software-based learning for C-Programming, Data Structures, and AutoCAD.

Software: MATLAB, Python, AutoCAD, Code Blocks, MiPower, and EMTP.

Microprocessor and Microcontroller Laboratory
Scope: Training in assembly language, interfacing programmable chips, and hardware/software integration.

Equipment: 8086 kits, 8051 kits, ARM Controller kits, and various interfacing modules (ADC/DAC, Stepper motors).

Lab Investment (Combined): Rs. 1,08,30,110/-

Location: Room No. A-306

Contact: Mr. B. Ganesh Babu (Asst. Prof) / Mr. B. Venkatesh & Mrs. S. Shilpa (Skilled Assts.)

Background: Established in 1995, utilized by UG/PG students and faculty for regular lab work and research projects.

Description: Facilities to measure Resistance, Inductance, and Capacitance. Also tests insulation strength of transformer oil.

Current Equipment: Dielectric Oil testing kit, CT/PT Test sets, Three-phase auto transformers, Potentiometers, and Energy meters.

Lab Investment: Rs. 10,35,868/-

Location: Room No. A-313

Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Contact: M. Naga Jyothi (Asst. Prof) / K. Radhika (Sr. Skilled Asst.)

8. Power Systems Laboratory
Background: Focuses on the analysis of relays, protection schemes, and power system circuits.

Description: Features transmission line models, synchronous machines, and various electromagnetic relays for B.Tech and M.Tech research.

Current Equipment: IDMT Over-current relays, Microprocessor-based UV/OV relays, Differential protection of transformers, and Under-ground cable testing setups.

Lab Investment: Rs. 15,98,295/-

Location: Room No. A-307

Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM

Contact: Mrs. Seelam Poornima (Asst. Prof) / Mr. B. Sivaji (Instructor)

Current Equipment: Solar PV training system, Three-phase IGBT inverters, Vector control drives, and Cycloconverters.
