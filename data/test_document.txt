The Department of Electrical & Electronics Engineering (EEE) offers U.G and P.G programs in Electrical Engineering Discipline. The department has a rich tradition of teaching and research and is widely recognized to be a pioneer in Electrical Engineering Education. The Department is recognized as a Research Centre in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. The research work in the department is vibrant with the support of eminent professors like Dr.M.Rama Moorthy, former Director General, CPRI taking a keen interest in guiding the faculty members. The research interests of the faculty members encompass a wide gamut of sub-disciplines of Electrical Engineering. The Department takes pride in its ability to nurture students who win gold medals at the university level consistently. We also take up the social responsibility of inculcating awareness about Energy Conservation by promoting programs about the same. Collaboration with industries for timely amendments of curriculum and laboratories is another credential of the department.

--- over all pacement summary ---

The placement data for the EEE department shows a concerning **downward trend** in both the volume of students placed and the premium salary opportunities over the three-year period (2023–2025).

### ## Placement Success Trends

The most striking insight is the steady decline in the placement rate. While the department started strong in 2023, it has faced significant challenges in the subsequent years.

| Metric | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Placement Rate** | ~83% | ~65.6% | ~54% |
| **Total Placed** | 108 | 82 | 76 |
| **Unplaced Students** | 18 | 43 | 62 |

---

### ## Salary Analysis (LPA)

The financial outlook for graduates has also contracted, particularly regarding high-tier offers.

* **Average Salary:** There has been a gradual dip from **5.49 LPA** in 2023 to **4.82 LPA** in 2025.
* **Highest Package:** The "ceiling" for EEE graduates dropped drastically from a high of **18.50 LPA** in 2023 to a plateau of **9.00 LPA** in 2024 and 2025. This suggests a loss of "super-dream" recruiters or premium roles.
* **Stability in the Core:** Despite the drop in high-end offers, the **Median Salary** has remained relatively stable around **4.50 LPA**, and the **Lowest Package** has actually improved slightly (rising from 2.40 LPA to 3.00 LPA). This indicates that the "base" level of recruitment remains consistent.

---

### ## Key Takeaways & Challenges

* **The "Unplaced" Gap:** The number of unplaced students has more than tripled (from 18 to 62). This suggests that while the curriculum aligns with a "core" group of recruiters, it may not be adapting fast enough to market shifts.
* **Higher Studies:** Interest in higher education remains very low (averaging only ~3-4 students per year), meaning the vast majority of the batch is entering the job market immediately.
* **Skill Shift Needed:** The reports consistently mention a need for **hybrid skill sets** (Software + Core) and emerging tech like **EV (Electric Vehicles)** and **Automation**. The stagnation in the 9 LPA cap suggests that graduates may need these specialized skills to break back into the 10+ LPA bracket.

### ## Overall Summary

The EEE department is currently in a **recessionary placement cycle**. While the "floor" for salaries is rising, the "ceiling" has fallen, and the total number of opportunities is shrinking. To reverse this, there is a clear mandate to pivot toward EV and automation training to attract higher-paying recruiters.
